Publications
Few-Shot Domain Adaptation for Effective Data Drift Mitigation in Network Management

S. S. Johari, M. Tornatore, R. Boutaba, A. Saleh
International Conference on Distributed Computing Systems (ICDCS) 2025.

This paper presents a novel domain adaptation approach for managing data drift in 5G and beyond networks, especially in few-shot scenarios with limited target domain data. By leveraging causal inference to isolate domain-invariant features and incorporating GANs to utilize domain-variant information, the method shows a 43.9% improvement over existing techniques in a network failure classification task.

Anomaly Detection and Localization in NFV Systems by Utilizing Masked-Autoencoder and XAI

S. S. Johari, N. Shahriar, M. Tornatore, R. Boutaba, A. Saleh
IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), November 2025.
Paper

This paper presents a novel anomaly detection and localization approach for NFV systems, addressing the challenges of training-data contamination and limited labeled data by combining a density-estimation teacher model with a Masked Autoencoder-based student model. The method improves anomaly detection accuracy and localization, outperforming existing techniques by up to 24% and 22% respectively in F1-score on NFV datasets, thereby enhancing reliability in mobile computing environments.

Active Learning for Transformer-Based Fault Diagnosis in 5G and Beyond Mobile Networks

S. S. Johari, N. Shahriar, M. Tornatore, R. Boutaba, A. Saleh
IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management(TNSM), September 2025.

This paper introduces an active learning framework specifically designed for Transformer-based fault diagnosis in 5G networks, reducing the dependency on large labeled datasets by selecting the most informative samples for annotation. By leveraging Transformers’ attention weights to detect novelty in sample processing patterns, the framework achieves higher F1-scores and significantly improves fault type identification with fewer labeled samples.

DRL-Assisted Reoptimization of Network Slice Embedding on EON-enabled Transport Networks

S. S. Johari, S. Taeb, N. Shahriar, S. R. Chowdhury, M. Tornatore, R. Boutaba, J. Meetra, M. Hemmati
IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management(TNSM), January 2023.
Paper

This paper tackles the issue of spectrum fragmentation in Elastic Optical Networks supporting 5G network slicing by periodically re-optimizing slice embedding using simulated annealing, a greedy algorithm, and Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) for optimal timing. The approach effectively improves slice acceptance rates and reduces the need for re-configuration actions, enhancing network efficiency and revenue potential for operators.

Anomaly Detection and Localization in NFV Systems: an Unsupervised Learning Approach

S. S. Johari, N. Shahriar, M. Tornatore, R. Boutaba, A. Saleh
NOMS 2022 (Recipient of the Best Student Paper Award)
Paper

This paper introduces a Noisy-Student-based anomaly detection approach for NFV systems, enhancing robustness against contaminated training data and leveraging it to improve detection accuracy. Additionally, it proposes an explainability-driven method for unsupervised anomaly localization, achieving up to 22% better detection and 19% better localization compared to existing techniques.
